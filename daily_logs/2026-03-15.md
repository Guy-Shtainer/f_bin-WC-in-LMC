## 18:59 — Statistical methodology discussion for instructor (KS vs CvM)

**Topics:** KS test limitations, weighted KS failure, p-value interpretation, CvM justification, bin size selection, parabolic interpolation filtering, neighborhood-of-minimum approach

**Work done:**
- Prepared detailed explanation for user to present to instructor (Tomer) covering:
  - Why classic KS (max|CDF diff|) is suboptimal for N=25 stars (low power, ignores shape)
  - Why weighting the D statistic per bin failed (Kolmogorov p-value formula invalid for weighted supremum)
  - How p-values are calculated (Kolmogorov series for KS, empirical permutation for CvM)
  - Why p-values are not for model ranking (uniform under null, designed for reject/don't-reject)
  - Why CvM is better (uses all bins, variance-weighted, empirical p-value always valid)
  - Minimum S = best fit (same principle as minimum χ²)
  - Optimal bin size: 10-20 km/s (current 10 km/s is fine, CDF smoothness makes it robust)
- Clarified p-value interpretation: p≈0.5 = typical draw (good), p<0.05 = outlier (reject), p>0.95 = suspiciously good fit (overestimated variance), healthy range 0.05-0.95
- Discussed parabolic interpolation filtering: neighborhood-of-minimum approach (N nearest points) as addition to existing height-based and range-based modes
- Started plan for neighborhood mode implementation but user triggered /EndConv before approval

**Decisions:**
- CvM S-score minimization is the correct approach for best-model selection (not p-value maximization)
- Both tails of p-value are suspicious: p<0.05 AND p>0.95 should be flagged
- Parabolic fit should use only nearby points, not entire grid (parabola is local approximation)
- Neighborhood mode to be ADDED alongside existing filtering modes (not replacing)
- Suggested default: ±2 neighbors (5 points in 1D)
