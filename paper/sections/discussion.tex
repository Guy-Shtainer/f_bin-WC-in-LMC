\subsection{Comparison with Galactic WC stars}
\label{sec:comparison_mw}

\citet{Dsilva2023} measured the observed binary fraction of Galactic WC/WO
stars to be $\sim 40\%$, applying the same cross-correlation methodology.
Our LMC result of $\fbinobs$ (observed) is broadly consistent, though a
direct comparison requires accounting for differences in detection
sensitivity, sample size, and the underlying orbital-parameter
distributions.

\subsection{Metallicity dependence}
\label{sec:metallicity}

At the sub-solar metallicity of the LMC ($Z \approx 0.3$--$0.5$\,\Zsun;
\citealt{Russell1992}), line-driven mass loss is weaker, raising the
minimum initial mass for single-star WR formation. This should increase
the relative contribution of the binary channel and, consequently, the
binary fraction \citep{Shenar2020}. Our measured intrinsic binary fraction
of $\fbincorr$ can be compared to the Galactic value from
\citet{Dsilva2023} to test this prediction.

\subsection{Formation channels}
\label{sec:formation}

The measured binary fraction places direct constraints on the relative
importance of single-star mass loss versus binary stripping in producing
WC/WO stars at LMC metallicity. A binary fraction significantly above
$50\%$ would support the hypothesis that binary interactions dominate WR
formation at sub-solar metallicity \citep{Marchant2023}.
