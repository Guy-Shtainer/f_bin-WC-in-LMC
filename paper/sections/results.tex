\subsection{Binary detections}
\label{sec:detections}

Applying the classification criteria described in
Sect.~\ref{sec:criteria} to the \primaryline radial velocities, we detect
\Ndetected new spectroscopic binaries among the \Nstars apparently single
targets. Combined with the \Nknown previously known binaries from
\citet{Bartzakos2001}, the total number of confirmed spectroscopic
binaries in the LMC WC/WO sample is \Nbin out of \Ntotal, yielding an
observed binary fraction of $\fbinobs$.

Table~\ref{tab:classification} lists the binary classification for each
star, including the maximum $\Delta\mathrm{RV}$, the associated
significance, and the epoch pair that triggered the detection.

\subsection{Observed binary fraction}
\label{sec:obs_fbin}

The observed binary fraction of $\fbinobs$ represents a lower limit on
the intrinsic binary fraction, as unfavourable orbital configurations
(long periods, low inclinations, low radial-velocity semi-amplitudes)
prevent detection for a fraction of the binary population. We address
this detection bias through Monte-Carlo simulations in
Sect.~\ref{sec:bias}.
